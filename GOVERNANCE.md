# Project Governance

This document outlines the governance structure, roles, and decision-making processes for this project.

## Decision-Making Process

The project operates primarily on a consensus-seeking model.

### Consensus Model
For most day-to-day operations (e.g., code reviews, bug fixes, minor feature additions), decisions are made through "lazy consensus." A change is accepted if it has been reviewed and approved by at least one maintainer, and no other maintainer has raised a blocking objection within a reasonable timeframe (typically 72 hours, excluding weekends).

### Escalation Path
If consensus cannot be reached on a significant technical or community decision, the project follows this escalation path:
1. **Extended Discussion:** Maintainers will hold a dedicated discussion (via issue, pull request, or meeting) to explore the objections in depth.
2. **Voting:** If consensus remains elusive, the active maintainers will hold a formal vote. A simple majority decides the outcome.
3. **Escalation to AAC:** If the vote is tied, or if the decision has a massive impact on the project's strategic direction, the issue escalated to the AAC for guidance.

## Relationship with the Foundation and AAC

This repository is governed as part of the Advanced Agentic Coding (AAC) organization and falls under the broader umbrella of the Foundation.

- **The Foundation:** Provides the legal framework, overarching policies, and administrative support for the project. While the Foundation does not directly manage the day-to-day technical direction of this specific repository, it holds the ultimate authority over overarching community guidelines (such as the Code of Conduct) and legal status.
- **AAC (Advanced Agentic Coding):** Acts as the strategic and technical steering body for this ecosystem. The AAC provides high-level guidance, ensures alignment across multiple related repositories, and serves as the primary escalation point for critical governance issues that the repository maintainers cannot resolve locally.
- **The Repository:** Operates autonomously on a day-to-day basis. The repository's maintainers make technical decisions, manage contributions, and drive the project forward, provided those actions align with the broader goals of the AAC and the Foundation.

## Maintainership

### Current Maintainers
Maintainers are community members who have demonstrated a commitment to the long-term success of the project. The current maintainers are listed in the `CONTRIBUTORS.md` file.

### Powers and Responsibilities
Maintainers have the following powers and responsibilities within the repository:
- Write (push) access to the repository.
- Authority to merge, close, or request changes on pull requests.
- Ability to triage, assign, and label issues.
- Responsibility to uphold and enforce the Code of Conduct.
- Power to configure repository settings, cut releases, and publish artifacts.
- Driving the technical direction and roadmap of the project.

### Granting Maintainership
Maintainership is granted through a consensus vote by the existing maintainers. The process is as follows:
1. Any existing maintainer can nominate a community member who has made substantial and ongoing contributions to the project (e.g., code, documentation, community support).
2. The nomination is discussed among the maintainers.
3. If consensus is reached, the nominee is formally invited to become a maintainer.

### Revoking Maintainership
Maintainership can be revoked or relinquished under the following circumstances:
- **Voluntary Resignation/Inactivity:** A maintainer who has been inactive for an extended period (e.g., 6+ months) may step down or be transitioned to an "Emeritus" status by the active maintainers.
- **Code of Conduct Violations:** Severe or repeated violations of the Code of Conduct are grounds for immediate revocation of maintainership.
- **Vote of No Confidence:** If a maintainer repeatedly acts against the best interests of the project or community, the remaining maintainers may vote to revoke their status. Revocation for reasons other than voluntary resignation requires a supermajority vote of the other active maintainers.
